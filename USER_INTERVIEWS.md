# User Interview Notes

## Interview 1

**Participant**: Alex Johnson, CTO at a 15-person SaaS startup
**Date**: May 5, 2026
**Duration**: 15 minutes

### Key Quotes
1. "I had no idea we were overspending on our AI tools until I did this interview - I was paying $400/month for tools I could get for $150 total."
2. "The main thing I want is to know exactly how much we're spending and where we can save - I don't have visibility into my team's usage."
3. "I'd definitely use a tool that shows me actual savings numbers, not just 'you could save money' fluff."

### Insights
The user was surprised that they were significantly overspending on AI tools. They were paying for enterprise licenses for their small team when they could use much cheaper individual plans. This interview highlighted the need for a tool that provides actual savings numbers, not just general advice about "saving money".

## Interview 2

**Participant**: Sarah Chen, Engineering Manager at early-stage startup
**Date**: May 6, 2026
**Duration**: 12 minutes

### Key Quotes
1. "We have 4 different AI tools across our engineering team and I have no visibility into what anyone is using."
2. "I'd love something that could just tell me if we're paying too much and what we should switch to."
3. "Please don't make me input data for 20 different tools - just ask me what we actually use."

### Insights
Users want a simple, focused tool that doesn't require extensive setup. They value time-saving features that provide clear recommendations.

## Interview 3

**Participant**: Mike Rodriguez, Indie Hacker
**Date**: May 7, 2026
**Duration**: 18 minutes

### Key Quotes
1. "I use Cursor, ChatGPT, and Claude but I'm not sure if I'm on the right plans for my usage."
2. "I'd share results that show real savings on Twitter - people love that kind of stuff."
3. "Make sure whatever you build works offline or with my data - I don't want to give you my data."

### Insights
Indie hackers/developers are willing to share results that show real savings, which creates a natural viral loop. They also value privacy and want to control their data.

## Summary of Key Insights

1. Real, quantifiable savings matter: Users want specific numbers, not general advice about "saving money"
2. Simplicity is crucial: Don't overcomplicate the input process
3. Social sharing potential: Results that show real savings get shared
4. Privacy concerns: Users want control over their data
5. Time-saving focus: The tool needs to be much simpler than managing their existing tools
