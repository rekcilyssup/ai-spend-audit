# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User Definition

### Primary Target User
- **Job Title**: CTOs and Engineering Managers at 5-50 person startups
- **Company Stage**: Early-stage startups to Series A/B companies with growing engineering teams
- **Pain Points**: 
  - Unnecessary overspending on AI tools without realizing it
  - Lack of visibility into team's AI tool usage
  - No benchmark for cost optimization

## User Discovery and Research

### Pre-Project Research
Before building the tool, I conducted interviews with:
1. Startup CTOs who manage engineering teams and tooling budgets
2. Engineering managers tracking team productivity
3. Indie hackers/contractors managing their own tooling costs

### User Research Insights
- Most users are unaware of their actual AI spending
- Teams often overpay for AI tools due to lack of awareness
- There's significant interest in tools that can help optimize software costs

## Marketing Channels

### High-Intent Channels
1. **Developer Communities**: 
   - Hacker News
   - Reddit (r/MachineLearning, r/webdev, r/javascript)
   - Dev-focused Discord/Slack communities
   - Indie hacker communities

2. **Professional Networks**:
   - LinkedIn posts targeting engineering managers
   - CTO/developer newsletters
   - Developer-focused podcasts

### Content & Distribution Strategy
- Publish "State of AI Spend 2026" report with industry benchmarks
- Create case studies showing real savings from audits
- Share before/after cost optimization examples
- Partner with developer-focused publications for launch coverage

## Viral Loop & Growth Mechanics

### Built-in Sharing
- Each audit generates a shareable URL with Open Graph tags
- Results are designed to be screenshot-friendly for social sharing
- Email capture after value delivery (not before)

### Referral System
- Users who save >$500/month get referral bonuses
- Early users become advocates through real savings
- Social proof through audit results shared on social media

## Launch Plan (First 30 Days)

### Week 1-2: Beta Testing
- Reach out to 5-10 trusted developers for feedback
- Fix critical issues and gather testimonials

### Week 3-4: Public Launch
- Launch on Product Hunt with compelling story
- Target 50+ audits in first week for social proof
- Post in relevant communities (Hacker News, Reddit, dev-focused forums)
- Reach out to developer influencers for initial feedback

## Traction Goals (Week 1)
- 100 completed audits
- 25 high-savings conversions (> $500/month) leading to consultations
- 50 email signups for the "notify on new optimizations" list
- 1000 unique visitors to the audit tool
